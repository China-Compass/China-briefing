How to book hotels in China

Use the right booking path before you waste time in the wrong app.

The key question is not “can I book this?” It is “which channel is least likely to fail with my passport, payment method, and travel date?”

Decision rule

Check these four things first

  1. Does the place require passport-based real-name booking?
  2. Is there a timed entry window or daily quota?
  3. Can an international card or English checkout work?
  4. What is the nearby backup if tickets fail?
Hotels

Default path: Trip.com, then hotel official site

Use Trip.com when you need English checkout and passport-friendly support. For international chains, compare the official site if loyalty or cancellation matters.

China travel FAQ

Fast answers for first-time independent travelers.

Short answers to the questions that usually come up before booking hotels, attractions, trains, and city days.

Can I travel in China without joining a tour?

Yes, especially in major cities. The hard parts are usually booking channels, payment setup, passport-based tickets, and live navigation.

Which app should I install first?

Start with Trip.com, Alipay, WeChat, a local map app, and DiDi. Add Dianping when you want better restaurant discovery.

Do attractions require passports?

Many paid or timed-entry attractions do. Keep your passport details consistent across tickets, hotels, and train bookings.

When should I avoid famous sights?

Major holidays, weekends, and midday peaks are the main risk windows. Use the forecast label to decide how much to simplify.

Practical tips

Prepare the small things that keep a local situation moving.

These are not sightseeing tips. They are the tiny operating details that help when a counter, restaurant, taxi, or attraction gate needs a quick answer.

At counters

Open the proof before your turn

Have passport, QR code, booking page, and Chinese venue name ready. Staff can move faster when they do not need to interpret a long explanation.

Restaurants

Be specific about food limits

Vegetable dishes may still use meat broth, oyster sauce, shrimp, pork fat, or garnish. Vegetarian and vegan requests need direct Chinese lines.

Transport

Save the return before leaving

Keep your hotel address in Chinese, nearest metro station, and a screenshot of the area. This matters most after dinner or in bad weather.

Culture

Expect efficient service

Busy staff may be direct rather than chatty. Short requests, clear screenshots, and a calm tone usually work better than over-explaining.
