Hotel booking guide

Book a China hotel you can actually check into.

The goal is not just finding a room. For a self-guided China trip, choose a hotel that works with your passport, your arrival time, your payment setup, and your daily route.

Quick Booking Rule

Default

Use Trip.com first

It is usually the clearest path for English checkout, passport details, and international traveler support.

Compare

Check official hotel sites

Useful for international chains, loyalty benefits, cancellation rules, and direct confirmation.

Verify

Confirm passport check-in

Choose hotels that clearly accept foreign passport guests, especially outside major international chains.

Start With the Least Confusing Channel

  • Trip.com: usually the easiest default because the flow is friendly to international travelers.
  • Ctrip: useful for deeper domestic inventory, but harder if you cannot read Chinese or verify local details.
  • Booking.com or hotel official sites: useful for international chains and loyalty-member bookings.
  • Local platforms: useful only if you can handle Chinese-language flows or have local help.

Check This Before You Pay

  1. Can the hotel check in a foreign passport holder?
  2. Is it near a useful metro line, not just one attraction?
  3. Can you cancel if your visa, flight, or itinerary changes?
  4. Is the price inflated by a public holiday, trade fair, concert, or school break?
  5. Will the hotel hold your room if your flight lands late at night?

How to Judge a Hotel Area

For an independent trip, the best hotel is often the one that makes every day simpler. Pick the area before you pick the room.

Easy

Central area, metro nearby, many international hotels, low passport check-in friction.

Acceptable

Good value or local atmosphere, but you need better transport planning.

Careful

Remote, event-priced, language-dependent, or risky if you arrive late.

Avoid

Unclear passport policy, poor transport access, or too much holiday friction.
